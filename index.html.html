About

“If a city is like a  human body and Central Business District (CBD) is like the heart, then the transportation network of that city is nothing less than the veins and arteries, which transport the life-sustaining nutrients from one part of the body to other and help the body to flourish.”

I believe that the key to the equilibrium between travel demand and supply is through pragmatic policies that can promote the use of public transportation and non-motorized transportation modes.

I am currently working as a Junior Research Fellow (JRF) in the Transportation Engineering Lab at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore. I am working on the project titled “The Kumbh Mela Experiment: Measuring and Understanding the dynamics of mankind’s largest crowd” with the aim to develop sophisticated methods and algorithms to aid planners and event managers in managing extremely large crowds.

I recently completed a project with the aim to understand the influence of activities and socio-economic/demographics on an individual’s propensity towards walking. I used the concept of acceptable trip distance and statistical modeling to understand an individuals’ travel behavior. I wish to develop as a researcher, working towards sustainable solutions to transportation problems faced by developing nations of the world.
